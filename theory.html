BEHIND THE TABLE

Flux is more than a restaurant — it's an edible conversation. Every course is inspired by Relational Dialectics Theory (RDT), a way of understanding relationships that sees them as dynamic, ever-shifting, and full of tensions that can't be permanently “solved” — only navigated. Rather than treating tension as conflict, RDT invites us to see it as the heartbeat of connection. In every relationship, there are opposing needs — closeness and space, openness and privacy, predictability and surprise. Flux serves these tensions to you, one course at a time, so you can taste them, talk about them, and perhaps see them in a new light.

WHAT IS RELATIONAL DIALECTS THEORY?

Relational Dialectics Theory (RDT) was developed by communication scholars Leslie Baxter and Barbara Montgomery in the 1980s. It proposes that relationships aren't static — they're ongoing conversations between contradictory but equally important desires. These contradictions are called dialectical tensions.

KEY IDEAS OF RDT:

1. Tensions are natural — They're not signs of something “wrong,” but a sign that both needs matter.
2. No permanent solutions — You don't “fix” tensions; you manage them over time.
3. Tensions exist in dialogue — We make sense of relationships through the conversations we have.
4. They're everywhere — RDT applies to romantic relationships, friendships, families, and even work partnerships.

CORE TENSIONS REPRESENTED IN THE MENU

Our nine-course tasting menu explores nine tensions commonly discussed in RDT:

Autonomy ↔ Connection - Independence vs. togetherness
Openness ↔ Closedness - Sharing vs. keeping things private
Predictability ↔ Novelty - Stability vs. excitement
Inclusion ↔ Seclusion - Socializing together vs. enjoying solitude
Revelation ↔ Concealment - Public sharing vs. keeping the relationship to yourselves
Expression ↔ Nonexpression - Speaking your mind vs. holding back
Ideal ↔ Real - Expectations vs. reality in relationships
Past ↔ Future - Honoring history vs. embracing change
Joy ↔ Sorrow - Celebrating highs while acknowledging lows

WHY IT MATTERS

Most of us are taught to “resolve” disagreements or “balance” needs as if there's a perfect midpoint. RDT teaches that healthy relationships can live in the stretch — where two opposing truths exist side-by-side.
Flux brings this concept off the page and onto your plate, giving you a shared experience you can literally chew on.
